Understanding Model Accuracy

What is Accuracy in Deep Learning?

Accuracy measures how often our model makes correct predictions. It's calculated as the ratio of correct predictions to the total number of predictions made.

Our Accuracy Formula

\[ \text{Accuracy} = \frac{\text{True Positives} + \text{True Negatives}}{\text{Total Samples}} \]

Additional Metrics

While accuracy is important, we also monitor:

  • Precision: Measures how many identified deepfakes were actually deepfakes
  • Recall: Measures how many actual deepfakes were correctly identified
  • F1 Score: Harmonic mean of precision and recall

Performance Analysis

Training Progress

Our model achieved 94.7% accuracy on the validation set after 50 epochs of training. The graph shows steady improvement in the first 20 epochs, followed by gradual refinement.

Key Observations

  • Rapid learning in early epochs (1-15) with accuracy jumping from 65% to 90%
  • Plateau phase (20-35 epochs) where additional training provided diminishing returns
  • Final refinement (35-50 epochs) where we achieved our peak performance

Comparative Performance
